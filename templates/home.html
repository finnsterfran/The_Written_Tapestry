Write short, write sharp

Tell a tale, tell of glory, tell some lies, tell a story!

"Short stories are tiny windows into other worlds and other minds and other dreams. They are journeys you can make to the farside of the universe and still be back in time for dinner." - Neil Gaiman

Short stories are self-contained works of prose fiction whose function is to impart a moral, capture a moment, or evoke a certain mood. Short stories are often more focused, as all the elements within - plot, character, pacing, story structure, and so on - must work together towards this common goal.

The Different Types of Short Stories

Anecdote

An anecdote is a brief and revealing account of something interesting and amusing, which usually tells a story about a real person and/or incident. They function much like parables: short narratives with a core moral lesson.

Drabble

A drabble is a short work of fiction precisely one hundred words in length. The purpose of the drabble is brevity, testing the author's ability to express interesting and meaningful ideas in a confined space.

Fable

A fable is a succinct story, in proose or verse, featuring anthropomorphic creatures to tell a story with a moral. Often the moral is explicitly told at the end of the story.

Feghoot

A humorous short story or vignette endng in a pun, where the story contains sufficient context to recognize the punning humor.

Flash Fiction

Very brief, shorter than 1,000 words, fictional piece of literature that still offers character and plot development. Possesses a unique literary quality in its ability to hint at or imply a larger story.

Frame Story

Also know as a frame tale or a nested narrative. It is a literary technique of placing a story within a story, for the purpose of introducing or setting the stage for a main narrative or series of short stories.

Story Sequence

Also called a short story cycle or a composite novel, this is a group of stort stories that work together to form a longer piece, while still functioning as complete short stories on their own.

Sketch

A shorter than average piece containing little or no plot. It can be merely a description of a character or a location.

Vignette

A short, impressionistic piece that focuses on capturing a single scene, character, idea, setting, or object. There is little emphasis on adhering to conventional theatrical or literacy structure, or story development.
